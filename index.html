Dogs

Border Collie

Border collies are active, working dogs. If confined without activity and company, theycan become unhappy and destructive. The breed is highly intelligent, learns quickly and responds well to praise.

Siberian Husky

Huskies are intelligent but somewhat independent and stubborn. They thrive on human company, but need firm, gentle training right from puppy hood. Sibes are friendly, fastidious, and dignified.

Golden Retreiver

Because golden retrievers are easy to please, they respond positively to obedience training. They complement this trait by being playful, affectionate and even-tempered.

French Poodle

Poodles are notorious for their intelligence and ease of training. They are lively, active, fun-loving and family dogs with a sense of the ridiculous.

Beagle

A cheerful, affectionate dog, the beagle prefers company. This breed can be highly destructive and bark excessively if left alone.

Yorkshire Terrier

The Yorkshire terrier is energetic, feisty and domineering, but also affectionate. It is considered a good apartment dog, but it can be stubborn with house training.
